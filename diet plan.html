Boost your immunity - Need of the hour


Vitamin C

Consuming foods high in vitamin C increases white blood cell production, which is key to fighting infection.

  • grapefruits, oranges, tangerines
  • sweet red pepper, broccoli
  • strawberries, kale, kiwifruit
Zinc

Immune system cells need zinc to function as they are intended. Zinc is a mineral that our body does not store or produce.

  • Shellfish (crab, clams, lobster,mussels)
  • Poultry (chicken,turkey),red meat
  • Beans,fortified cereals,breads
Beta-Carotene

Beta-carotene converts into vitamin A, which is an anti-inflammatory vitamin that can help your antibodies respond to toxins, such as a virus.

  • Carrots, spinach
  • kale, apricots, sweet potato
  • squash,cantaloupe
Vitamin E

Vitamin E is a fat-soluble vitamin that is key in regulating and supporting immune system function.

  • nuts, seeds
  • spinach
  • avocado
Antioxidants

Green tea is packed with antioxidants that have been shown to enhance immune system function. It also contains amino acids that may aid in the production of germ-fighting compounds in your T-cells, which reduces inflammation in the body and helps fight infection.

  • Green tea
